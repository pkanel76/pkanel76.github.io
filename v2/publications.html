PUB-02 · MSc thesis

The Course of the Inflammatory Response During a Long-Duration Endurance Race

P. Kanellopoulos · MSc thesis, Democritus University of Thrace · 2021

An MSc thesis in exercise physiology tracking, hour by hour, how the body's inflammatory response builds during a six-hour endurance run at moderate intensity (50–60% VO₂max). Twelve moderately trained runners were blood-sampled before the start and at hourly intervals during the run, following cytokines (IL-6, IL-10, TNF-α), acute-phase proteins (CRP, SAA) and markers of muscle and cardiac damage (CPK, LDH, troponin T). IL-10, IL-6 and the muscle-damage marker CPK each showed a distinct inflection, the switch from gradual to steep accumulation, at the second, third and fourth hour respectively, while C-reactive protein and cardiac troponin T stayed undetectable throughout, pointing to substantial skeletal-muscle stress without measurable cardiac strain.

PUB-03 · Conference paper

Measurement of Inflammatory Markers IL-6, C-RP and CPK Before, During and After a 6-Hour Endurance Race

P. Kanellopoulos, I. Zacharogiannis, G. Paradisis, S. Batsiou, T. Pylianidis · 24th International Congress of Physical Education & Sport (EGVE) · Physical Education & Sport 41(2), 120 · 2021

A conference paper presenting part of the thesis work above: the before / during / after profile of interleukin-6 (IL-6), C-reactive protein (C-RP) and creatine kinase (CPK) in twelve amateur endurance runners over a six-hour run at 50–60% VO₂max, with blood drawn hourly. IL-6 climbed steeply from the outset, already up roughly 500% by the second hour and more than 3,000% by the finish, while CPK rose steadily to about 290% above baseline; C-reactive protein, a slower-acting acute-phase protein, did not shift across the six hours. The pattern points to both systemic inflammation and skeletal-muscle damage setting in early, detectable from the second hour of moderate-intensity effort.
